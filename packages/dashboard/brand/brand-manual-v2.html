Heritage
"Consider a future device... in which an individual stores all his books, records, and communications, and which is mechanized so that it may be consulted with exceeding speed and flexibility."
Vannevar Bush / "As We May Think" / The Atlantic, July 1945
1945 — THE ORIGINAL VISION

Bush envisioned the Memex as a desk-sized device that could store an entire library's worth of information, allowing instant cross-referencing through associative trails. It was the intellectual precursor to hypertext, the web, and modern knowledge management.

2026 — THE MODERN REINTERPRETATION

We build on Bush's vision: a persistent memory layer that connects knowledge across projects, sessions, and team members. Not a desk — a network. Not for one person — for teams and their AI agents.
