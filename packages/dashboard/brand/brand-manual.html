01 — Heritage & Mission

Heritage

In 1945, Vannevar Bush published "As We May Think" in The Atlantic. He described a device called the memex — a desk with screens, a keyboard, and buttons that would store and connect all of a person's documents, creating "trails" between related ideas.

Bush imagined augmenting human knowledge. Eighty years later, that vision is finally achievable — not just for individuals, but for entire teams, and not just for humans, but for AI agents.

Mission

Make every piece of team knowledge instantly accessible to both humans and AI agents.

Vision

A world where switching projects takes seconds, onboarding takes hours instead of weeks, and no knowledge walks out the door when someone leaves.

Tagline — Primary

"Your team's knowledge layer"

Tagline — Secondary

"What your agents know"

03 — Competitive Positioning

What Memex Is Not

Not a Wiki

Notion, Confluence — those are for humans only. Agents can't read them natively.

×

Not a Docs Generator

GitBook, Mintlify — those document APIs. We document knowledge.

×

Not a PM Tool

Linear, Jira — they track tasks. We track understanding.

×

What Memex Is

The knowledge layer that lives in git, readable by both humans and AI agents.

A dashboard that shows what your AI tools know (and don't know) about your codebase.

Infrastructure, not a destination — it works in the background.

Positioning Statement

For engineering teams using AI coding agents, Memex is the knowledge infrastructure that ensures your agents have the context they need. Unlike wikis or docs generators, Memex lives in your git repos and is natively readable by both humans and machines.

The 60 / 30 / 10 Rule

60% Surface
30% Text
10%
  • 60% — Surface (#131313 family): the void
  • 30% — On Surface (#e5e2e1, #c6c6c6): text and content
  • 10% — Tertiary (#22c55e): AI signals and actions

Green Usage Rules

Green is reserved for three categories. Overusing it devalues its signal.

AI indicators — agent activity, confidence scores, AI-generated content markers
Health & success — passing checks, healthy metrics, positive deltas
Active states — selected nav items, active filters, focused inputs

Maximum: 10% of any screen area. If it feels like more, pull back.

Depth & Shadows

Surface Layering

Primary method. Depth is achieved through background color shifts, not drop shadows. A card on Surface Container (#201f1f) placed on Surface Low (#1c1b1b) reads as "raised."

Ghost Border

Outline Variant at 15% opacity

Felt, not seen. Use when color shift alone is insufficient.

Ambient Lift (modals only)

Massive blur (40-60px) at 6% opacity using #e5e2e1 as tint. Reserved for floating modals and command palettes.

Glass Panels

Floating utility bar

Surface Container at 60% opacity + blur(12px). No border needed.

The No-Line Rule: Solid 1px borders for sectioning are prohibited. Use background color shifts. Ghost borders only for input fields and accessibility requirements.

09 — Brand Voice

Voice & Tone

Tone Spectrum

Context Tone
Dashboard UI Clinical, precise — data speaks
Error messages Direct, helpful — no fluff
Marketing copy Confident, understated — quiet authority
Documentation Technical, thorough — respect the reader
Onboarding Warm but efficient — don't waste time

Do

  • Use technical terms without explaining them
  • Lead with data, not adjectives
  • Be specific: "87% coverage across 12 projects"
  • Use active voice and short sentences

Don't

  • Use buzzwords: "leverage", "synergy", "game-changer"
  • Use emojis in the product UI
  • Explain what developers already know
  • Be casual or jokey — confidence, not comedy

Copy Examples

Before

"Supercharge your team's productivity with our amazing AI-powered knowledge solution!"

After

"Your agents read 12 repos. They understand 87% of the codebase. Here's what they're missing."

Before

"Oops! Something went wrong. Don't worry, we're on it!"

After

"Git sync failed for acme/api. Last successful sync: 4h ago. Retry or check SSH keys."
