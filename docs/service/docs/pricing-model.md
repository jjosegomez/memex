# Pricing Model

> Internal document. Pricing logic, tiers, and negotiation guidelines.

---

## Market Context (March 2026)

Based on research of comparable services:

| Comparable Service | Market Price Range |
|---|---|
| DX audit + optimization (DevRel Bridge, etc.) | $12K-$40K per engagement |
| AI tool setup for small teams (5-15 devs) | $5K-$15K one-time |
| Ongoing advisory retainer (specialized) | $5K-$20K/month |
| Fractional CTO (includes tooling) | $7.5K-$25K/month |
| Per-developer training/onboarding | $50-$100/developer |
| AI tool licenses alone (Copilot, Cursor, Claude) | $19-$39/user/month |

**Key gap:** Companies pay for AI tool licenses but struggle with setup, configuration, and adoption — which costs 3-6x the license fees. No one is prominently packaging this as a standalone service. Our positioning fills this gap at a price point below full DX consulting but above DIY.

**Our positioning:** Premium for solo consulting, competitive vs. agencies/firms. Growth tier ($5,500 setup) is intentionally below market ($12K+) to reduce friction for first clients. Room to increase as case studies and referrals build credibility.

---

## Pricing Philosophy

- **Charge for value, not hours.** The value scales with team size and project count — a 20-dev agency gets 20x the value from the same setup.
- **Low barrier to entry.** Discovery is cheap and credits toward setup. Minimize friction to get started.
- **Retainer is the business model.** Setup is a one-time revenue event. The retainer is where long-term revenue lives.
- **Price by tier, not by hour.** Clients understand tiers. Hourly billing incentivizes slow work and penalizes efficiency.
- **First clients are priced to close, not to maximize.** Get case studies and referrals. Raise prices after proving the model with 2-3 clients.

## Negotiation Guidelines

### Things you CAN flex on:
- Number of projects in initial setup (add 1-2 more without changing price if it closes the deal)
- Retainer consultation hours (offer +2 hrs/month for first 3 months as a trial incentive)
- Payment terms (net-30 instead of upfront, 50/50 split for setup)

### Things you should NOT flex on:
- Discovery fee (it's already credited — if they won't pay $500, they're not serious)
- 3-month minimum (tuning requires time; shorter commitments don't show value)
- Retainer price (this is your recurring revenue; discounting it compounds negatively)
- Scope of setup work (if they want more projects, charge for them)

### Discount Strategy
- **Annual commitment** (12 months): 10% off monthly retainer
- **Referral**: 1 month free retainer for each referred client that signs
- **Case study rights**: 5% off setup if they let you publish a case study
